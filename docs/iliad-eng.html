vesum021

  1. Thus answer’d mild the cloud-compelling Jove.
  2. Nor God, nor₁ mortal shall our joys behold,
  3. Shaded with clouds, and circumfus’d in gold,
  4. Not ev’n the sun, who darts thro’ heav’n his rays,
  5. And whose broad eye th’ extended earth surveys.
  6. Gazing he spoke, and kindling at the view,
  7. His eager arms around the Goddess threw.
  8. Glad earth perceives, and from her bosom pours
  9. Unbidden herbs, and voluntary flow’rs;
  10. Thick new-born vi’lets a soft carpet spread,
  11. And clust’ring Lotos swell’d the rising bed,
  12. And sudden hyacinths the turf bestrow,
  13. And flamy Crocus made the mountain glow.
  14. There golden clouds conceal the heav’nly pair,
  15. Steep’d in soft joys, and circumfus’d with air;
  16. Celestial dews, descending o’er the ground,
  17. Perfume the mount, and breathe Ambrosia round.
  18. At length with love and sleep’s soft pow’r opprest,
  19. The panting Thund’rer nods, and sinks to rest.
